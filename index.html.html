Kerala 50+ Multidisciplinary Talent, Pharmacy & Care Ecosystem

Prototype Report for community service, professional activation, business development, and sustainable revenue generation in Kerala

SMART | Simple | Doable | Theory-backed | Stakeholder-focused | Revenue-driven

1. Executive Summary

This prototype proposes a Kerala-based multidisciplinary ecosystem that activates experienced professionals above 50 years of age, including pharmacists and allied healthcare, business, and service professionals, into trusted work, care, sales, marketing, business development, and service opportunities.

The model integrates community pharmacy, clinical pharmacy, home care, research, education, pharmaceutical sales, PCD company support, and service coordination into a single digital and operational framework. It is designed to solve three major problems: underutilized senior expertise, fragmented access to trusted services, and lack of structured matching between professionals and opportunities.

The platform is financially sustainable through a pure revenue model based on successful matching, subscriptions, service commissions, referrals, enterprise contracts, and training services.

2. Problem Statement

Problem 1: Underused Expertise

Many qualified professionals above 50 remain partially active, underemployed, or excluded from structured opportunities.

Problem 2: Fragmented Services

Users struggle to find trusted pharmacists, care providers, business support, and service professionals in one place.

Problem 3: Weak Opportunity Matching

There is no simple system to connect skills, availability, location, and experience with suitable roles and services.

3. Theory and Validation Base

  • Social Enterprise Theory: The model creates social value and revenue simultaneously.
  • Human Capital Theory: Experienced professionals have high-value knowledge that remains economically useful.
  • Platform Theory: Digital matching reduces search cost and improves trust between users and providers.
  • Stakeholder Theory: The model creates value for professionals, users, employers, and partners.
  • Service-Dominant Logic: Value is created through service interaction, not only product sale.
This model is validated by theory because it treats experience as an asset, builds a trust-based platform, and creates value through service delivery and business development.

4. Vision, Mission, and SMART Objective

Vision

To create a trusted Kerala ecosystem where experienced professionals continue to serve society through meaningful, dignified, and paid multidisciplinary roles.

Mission

To connect pharmacists and allied professionals above 50 with opportunities in healthcare, pharmacy, business development, sales, marketing, research, education, and service sectors through a structured and sustainable platform.

SMART Objective

Within 12 months, build and pilot a Kerala-based platform that registers at least 200 verified professionals, enables 50 successful service or work matches, and generates recurring revenue through at least 3 monetization streams.

H. Employment Logic of the Framework

This framework is designed on the principle that experience does not retire with age. Instead, professional value can be redirected into roles that are flexible, location-sensitive, dignity-preserving, revenue-generating, and socially impactful.

The platform creates a bridge between talent and demand by combining pharmacy knowledge, healthcare service delivery, business growth functions, and community engagement.

I. Expected Outcome of the Framework

Through this framework, the ecosystem can realistically create 50+ job and engagement pathways, 20+ pharmacy-specific roles, 15+ sales and marketing opportunities, 10+ business development and PCD roles, and multiple entrepreneurial and digital service pathways.

This makes the model financially relevant, socially useful, and strategically aligned with Kerala’s healthcare and pharmaceutical market structure.

8. Revenue Model

The revenue model is built to be pure, repeatable, and linked to user satisfaction.

Matching Fees

Fee collected when a professional is successfully placed in a role or project.

Subscriptions

Premium profiles, employer search access, and advanced matching tools.

Commission on Services

Small commission from completed telepharmacy, home care, sales support, or advisory services.

Referral Revenue

Income from referrals to labs, insurance, wellness, training, and partner services.

Enterprise Contracts

Institutional packages for hospitals, NGOs, CSR projects, pharma companies, and care programs.

Training / Certification

Fees from refresher courses, digital upskilling, continuing education, and sales training programs.
